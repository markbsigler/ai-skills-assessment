# AI Skills Assessment for Product Managers & Product Owners

## Product Management | Self-Assessment Survey

> **Version:** 0.4.0 | **Last Updated:** 2025-06-01

**Instructions:** For each dimension, select the score (0–9) that best describes your current capability. Be honest — this assessment is designed to identify growth opportunities, not to evaluate performance. Scores are defined by the highest level you can perform *consistently and independently*.

> **Note on examples:** Benchmark tasks reference our primary PM toolchain — **M365 Copilot** for drafting and meeting summaries, **GitHub Copilot** for reviewing engineering specs, **Claude** for deep analysis and synthesis, **Jira** for backlog and sprint management, **Aha!** for roadmapping and strategy, **xWiki** for knowledge management, **Miro** for whiteboarding and workshops, and **Figma** for design collaboration — but are *illustrative, not prescriptive*. The AI tooling landscape evolves rapidly; substitute current equivalents when self-assessing.

## What to Focus on Next

Use these heuristics to prioritize your development:

1. **Any dimension below 3?** Start there. Foundational gaps limit your ability to benefit from AI in daily product work.
2. **Safety below your other scores?** Close that gap next. High capability without risk awareness creates liability — especially when handling customer data and strategic information.
3. **Product Definition lagging Prompt Engineering?** Strong prompting without strong product definition skills means you're generating text, not shipping better products. These should grow together.
4. **Analytics lagging Market Intelligence?** Data-driven decisions require both gathering insights and rigorously analyzing them. Balance research skills with analytical rigor.
5. **Communication lagging other scores?** Product management is a communication-heavy discipline. Even strong analysis and strategy lose impact if stakeholder communication lags behind.
6. **Collaboration below 3 while other scores are 5+?** You're a capable individual contributor but not yet a team multiplier. Sharing your practices will have outsized impact.
7. **Widest gap between dimensions?** Balance your profile — a well-rounded PM contributes more than a narrow specialist.
8. **All dimensions 5+?** Focus on the areas most relevant to your current role and product challenges, and begin mentoring others.
9. **All dimensions 7+?** Shift toward organizational impact — define standards, build shared resources, and lead adoption.
